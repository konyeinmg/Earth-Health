<h1 style="text-align:center">How Do We Save The World?</h1>
<h2>How To Protect Air Pollution</h2><br>
<h4>On The Road</h4>
<p style="text-indent:3em">About half of air pollution comes from cars and tracks.Fewer trips in your car or track can help improve air quality.Even how you drive can reduce your car's footprint.Take these into consideration when you are travelling on roads:</p>
<ul type="square">
<li>Walk or ride a bike when possible.</li>
<li>Take Public transportation.</li>
<li>Organize and condense errands into one trip.</li>
<li>When driving, accelerate gradually and obey the speed limit.</li>
<li>Drive less , particulary on days with unhealthy air .</li>
<li>Maintain your vehicle and keep your tires properly inflated.</li>
<li>Support the Smog Check Program.</li>
<li>Report smoking vehicles to respective organizations.</li>
<li>Travel lightly and and remove any unnessary items that may weight down your vehicle.</li>
<li>Limit idling your vehicle to no more than 30 seconds.</li>
<li>When in the market for the new car , look for the most efficient , <br>lowest-polluting vehicle or even a zero-emission electric car.</li>
</ul>
<h4>At Home</h4>
<p style="text-indent:3em">There are many ways air quality can be improved in the home.By reducing energy consumption , choosing sustainable products and eliminating your exposure to chemicals  , we can all contribute to a cleaner World.</p>
<p style="text-indent:3em">Reducing energy consumption helps reduce air pollution.If less gasoline  , natural gas and electricity (power plants burn fossil fuels to generate electricity) are used , not only do your bills decrease but less pollutants are emitted.</p>
<p style="text-indent:3em">There are many products in the home , garden and garage that emit smog-forming chemicals that pollute the air we used.Volatile organic compounds(VOCs) and particulate matter(PM) contained in these products penetrate deep into the lung and can trigger asthma attacks or worsen respiratory illnesses.The suggestions below will help reduce exposure in your home:</p>
<ul type="square">
<li>Turn the lights off when you leave a room.</li>
<li>Replace energy-hungry incandescent lights with compact florescent light bulbs.</li>
<li>Ask your energy suppliers for a home audit and inquire about<br>alternative energy solutions like solar or wind.</li>
<li>Opt for a fan instead of air condinition.</li>
<li>Use a programmable thermostat and set it to 78 degree Fahrenheit in the summer and 68 Degree Fahrenheit in the winter.</li>
<li>Install low-flow shower heads.</li>
<li>Recycle paper , plastics , metals and organic materials.</li>
<li>Use EPA-approved wood burning stove or fireplace on days insert.</li>
<li>Don't use your wood stove or fireplace on days with unhealthy air.</li>
<li>Donit heat your home with a gas stove.</li>
<li>Use a surge protector for multiple appliance and turn it off when products are not in use.</li>
<li>Add insulation to your home.</li>
<li>Wash laundry in cold water and line dry.</li>
<li>When ready to replace, look for energy star appliances.</li>
<li>Use a propane or natural gas barbecue rather than a charcoal one.</li>
<li>Microwave or use a toaster oven for small meals.</li>
<li>Have your gas appliances and heater regularly inspected and maintained.</li>
<li>Use washable dishes, utensils and fabric napkins rather than disposable dinnerwave.</li>
<li>Choose products that use recycled materials.</li>\
<li>Eat locally,shop at farmers markets and buy organic products.</li>
<li>Buy products from sustainable sources such as bamboo and hemp.</li>
<li>Use durable reusable grocery bags and keep them in your car so you're never caught off guard.</li>
<li>Paint with a brush instead of a sprayer.</li>
<li>Store all solvents in airlight containers.</li>
<li>Use an electric or push lawn owner.</li>
<li>Use a rake or broom instead of a leaf blower.</li>
<li>Use water-based cleaning products that labeled zero VOC.</li>
<li>Insulate your water heater and any accessible hot water pipes.</li>
<li>Elimate use of toxic chemicals at home; opt for natural substitutes.</li>
<li>Plant a tree!They filter the air and provide shade.</li>
<li>Let your elected representatives know you support action for cleaner air.</li>
